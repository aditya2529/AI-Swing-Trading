_Deeper wide-net deep research — 15 agents (10 family surveys + 4 adversarial fact-checks + synthesis). 2026-06-06._

You asked for blunt. Every realistic money-making approach in markets, ranked by what a retail Indian trader can **actually keep** after costs, taxes, slippage, and behaviour.

**The single most important number:** SEBI confirms **91% of individual F&O traders lost money in FY25, aggregate net losses Rs 1.05 lakh crore (~Rs 1.1 lakh per person).** Fact-checked & confirmed (SEBI study + Parliament). Almost everything that *feels* like "trading" is on the losing side of that line.

## Illusions sold to retail
- **F&O premium selling / "consistent income."** VRP is real but harvested by prop algos profitable >97% of the time. You are the counterparty. SEBI's 91% loss rate *is* these traders.
- **Telegram tips / "high-accuracy" algo subs / guaranteed returns.** Negative EV before you trade; repeatedly SEBI-flagged as fraud.
- **Smallcases / median thematic baskets.** Backtested, survivorship-prone, gross returns; live they trail a plain index after rebalance STCG + slippage + fees.
- **Sectoral/thematic NFOs (PSU, defence, infra).** Launched after the theme has run; ~5% behaviour gap dominates.
- **Active funds "beating the market."** SPIVA India: ~73% large-cap, ~82% mid/small underperform over 10yr; winners unpickable ex-ante.
- **"Small-cap outperformance."** Size premium barely exists after liquidity/quality controls; Indian small-cap indices carry survivorship bias.
- **Day trading / TA as a living.** Best-documented loss in finance; ~1% reliably profitable.

## The fact-checks (all 4 confirmed)
SEBI 91% F&O loss / ₹1.05L cr — confirmed. SPIVA active-fund underperformance — confirmed. India momentum alpha concentrated in illiquid stocks — confirmed. Smallcases/PMS fail to beat cheap index net of costs — confirmed.

## Where a small retail trader's time + capital should actually go
A simple, automated, diversified core: roughly **60–70% Indian broad/multi-factor index SIP** (tilt **low-vol + quality**), **20–30% global (S&P 500 / world)** for diversification, and a **5–10% real-asset sleeve (gold ETF + REITs)**. Optionally a 10–20% managed-futures/trend sleeve if accessible, for crisis protection. Automate it, then *do nothing for years* — the hardest, highest-paid skill is sitting through drawdowns without selling. Spend energy on guaranteed wins: savings rate, direct plans, tax efficiency, never paying for tips. Treat F&O, intraday, small-cap punting, and subscriptions as entertainment with near-certain negative expectancy — cap at "fun money." For retail, the winning move is boring, cheap, diversified, patient; almost every exciting alternative is someone else's business model feeding on your costs, taxes, and impatience.
