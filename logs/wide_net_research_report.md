# The Honest Retail Trader's Scorecard (India, 2026)

_Wide-net deep research — every realistic retail money-making approach in markets, ranked honestly. 5-agent light run, 2026-06-06._

Bottom line up front: almost everything sold to you as a "strategy" loses to a cheap index fund after costs, taxes, and your own behaviour. Below is every approach ranked by what a real retail trader can actually keep.

## The Illusions Sold to Retail
- **F&O "income"** — naked option selling, credit spreads, iron condors. 9 in 10 lose; the edge goes to prop algos. You are the counterparty.
- **Telegram tips / "high-accuracy" algo subscriptions** — negative EV by design; many SEBI-flagged scams.
- **Small-cap "outperformance"** — survivorship-bias + illiquidity illusion dressed as a premium.
- **Active funds & most smallcases / dividend strategies** — fee-and-tax leaks that quietly lose to the index.
- **Headline momentum CAGR** — real number, untradeable for retail (alpha locked in illiquid stocks).
- **PMS "beats the market"** — gross, bull-window, wrong-benchmark marketing.

## Where a small retail trader's time and capital should actually go
Put 80–100% in a cheap, direct-plan **broad-market or multi-factor index fund via SIP**; tilt the core toward **low-volatility and quality** for a modest durable edge; then *do nothing* — sitting through -50% drawdowns without selling is the hardest, most valuable skill. If you want diversification and can stomach years of looking wrong, add a 10–20% **managed-futures/trend ETF** sleeve. Spend "trading" energy on guaranteed wins: savings rate, direct plans, tax efficiency, never paying for tips. Treat F&O, small-cap punting, and subscriptions as entertainment with near-certain negative expectancy — cap at "fun money" you can lose entirely. The uncomfortable truth: for retail, the winning move is to be boring, cheap, and patient, because almost every exciting alternative is someone else's business model feeding on your costs, taxes, and impatience.
