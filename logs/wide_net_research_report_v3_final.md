## Fact-checks
- SEBI 91% F&O loss / ₹1.05 lakh crore FY25 (up 41% YoY) — **CONFIRMED** (SEBI + Parliament).
- Active funds underperform (73% large-cap, 82% mid/small over 10yr, SPIVA) — **CONFIRMED**.
- Momentum alpha concentrated in illiquid stocks — **CONFIRMED**.
- PMS/smallcases fail to beat cheap index net of costs — **PARTLY** (central tendency yes; a rare top-quartile can, but unpickable ex-ante).
- India crypto tax structurally unprofitable for active trading — **CONFIRMED**.
- IPO listing gains strong but long-run underperformance + rationed allotment — **CONFIRMED**.
- Behaviour gap (investor return < fund return by a few pp/yr) — **CONFIRMED**.

## The final plan
1. **Max the guaranteed-return buckets first:** EPF/VPF (8.25% tax-free) and PPF — unbeatable risk-free.
2. **Core growth (automated SIP):** ~60–70% Indian **index / multi-factor**, tilted **low-vol + quality**; **20–30% global** (S&P 500 / world); **5–10% gold ETF + REITs**.
3. **Cash/goals:** arbitrage + liquid + target-maturity funds.
4. **Automate everything, then do nothing for years.** The behaviour gap (panic-selling, overtrading) costs more than fund selection — process is the edge.
5. **Avoid the entire trap list.** F&O, intraday, leverage, smallcases, tips, active crypto = statistically negative EV. Cap any of it at "fun money" you can lose entirely.

For retail, the winning move is boring, cheap, diversified, patient. Almost every exciting alternative is someone else's business model feeding on your costs, taxes, and impatience.
